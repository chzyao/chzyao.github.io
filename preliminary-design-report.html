Project Overview

This project followed the conceptual design of a 90-passenger regional jet and focused on the structural design and analysis of its primary airframe components ‚Äî wings, fuselage, and empennage ‚Äî under multiple load cases, including symmetric flight, One-engine-inoperative (OEI), and nose-off landing. Our team developed analytical models for shear, bending, and torsion, optimised component weight and stiffness, and evaluated composite versus metallic configurations for manufacturability and fatigue resistance.

I was responsible for the fuselage structural design, including the sizing of skin, stringers, and frames under pressurisation and bending loads. My work involved deriving load distributions, performing buckling and stability checks using analytical and numerical methods, and conducting material selection and optimisation through CES EduPack (now known as Ansys Granta EduPack) and MATLAB.
